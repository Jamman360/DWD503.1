Our Roots

Originally discovered by Polynesian seafarers between 1280 and 1350, and later by the famous Abel Tasman in 1642, New Zealand was the last large habitable land mass to be settled. almost 200 years later, history was made when The British Empire signed a treaty with the Maori Chiefs. This made New Zealand a colony under British sovereignty, later gaining independence in 1907, with the British Monarch as Head of State.

The Treaty

The Treaty of Waitangi was a document drafted by representatives of the british settlers, and translated into Maori to be circulated around different Maori settlements around the North Island. The Translation at the time was the best they could manage, but there were several parts where the Maori translation was not the most accurate, and caused issues later on down the line.

One thing the treaty allowed the settlers to do was to purchase land from the Maori People, and while this was honored some of the time, there were equal if not more instances of unfair deals, occupation without sale of land, or even outright confiscations after the New Zealand Wars around 1850, where the Government held the Treaty in complete disregard.

Only around 1950, as Maori increasingly began to use the Treaty as a platform to reclaim land and rights, did the Government begin to listen to them, and start to bring the Treaty into a more central place in NZ Law. 1975 brought around the Treaty of Waitangi Act, establishing a Waitangi Tribunal to handle all related inquiries and advise the Government on actions to take that keeps the Treaty in mind.

A Brief History of our Mining Past

With a plethora of historical mining sites around the country, Mining has been a large part of our culture. New Zealand ranks among the top 30 countries for Iron and Gold ore production, and the mining sector alone in 2017 contributed to 1.3% of the GDP that year. There have been 3 major Gold Rushes - One in Coromandel in 1852, and the Otago and West Coast Rushes both in the 1860s. Mining techniques evolved over the years, from panning gold to dredging and using Stamper Batteries to get gold out of quartz veins. Relatively recently there has been opposition to proposals to restart mining in the Coromandel Peninsula, where one of our Countries' largest gold deposits is.
